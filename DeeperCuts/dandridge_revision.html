US Electric Power Generation

Biomass, Geothermal, and Solar Sources

Map created by Harrison Dandridge

This map displays alternative energy power plants across the US. Symbol size represents generation capacity in megawatts (MW).

Design Principle Used: Incorporating shapes to de-clutter the map

What Changes Were Made: Distinct shapes were assigned to each energy source—circles for Biomass, triangles for Geothermal, and squares for Solar. Additionally, I implemented an interactive filter that allows users to toggle the visibility of smaller plants (under 50 MW), reducing visual clutter at the national scale.

Why This Map is Better: By using varied shapes instead of identical circles, users can better distinguish between energy types without relying solely on color, which improves accessibility and intuitiveness. The filter control gives users the ability to focus on major power plants when the full dataset feels overwhelming, creating a cleaner, more insightful viewing experience that adapts to different audience needs.
